CAI in the New Curriculum in Uganda

Continuous Assessment Integration (CAI) is a core component of Uganda’s new curriculum, emphasizing consistent evaluation of student skills and competencies. Unlike traditional exams, CAI tracks progress through projects, classroom activities, and practical exercises. With Shuleni Report Card System, schools can record and analyze CAI scores efficiently, ensuring accurate and comprehensive report cards.

Why CAI Matters

CAI helps teachers assess students’ abilities beyond exams. It ensures that learners develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity. Schools using Shuleni can easily integrate CAI scores into reports and provide meaningful feedback to parents.

Implementing CAI in Schools

  • Schedule regular projects and classroom tasks that align with competencies.
  • Track student participation and skill development consistently.
  • Use Shuleni to calculate and compile CAI results automatically.
  • Communicate progress to parents using detailed report cards.

Benefits of Using Shuleni for CAI

  • Reduces manual errors in grading
  • Saves teachers time on report preparation

  • Aligns with NCDC’s new curriculum requirements

Conclusion

CAI is essential for developing well-rounded learners in Uganda. By leveraging Shuleni Report Card Management System, schools can efficiently manage assessments, improve feedback, and ensure students achieve their full potential.
